Dr. Dora Akunyili

Nigeria's best NAFDAC Director-General

Here's a time line of Dr. Dora Akunyili's life

  • 1954: Dora was born in Benue,Makurdi state.

  • 1966: She receives her First School Leaving Certificate from St. Patrick's Primary School, Isuofia, Anambra State.

  • 1973: She has her West African School Certificate Examination (W.A.S.C) at Queen of the Rosary Secondary School Nsukka, Enugu State where she graduates with a Grade I Distinction.

  • 1978: Graduates from the University of Nigeria,Nsukka with a degree in pharmacology.

  • 1985: Receives her Ph.D. in ethnopharmacology.

  • 1981-1996: She becomes a Graduate Assistant in the FaU.NN. of Pharmaceutical Sciences, U.N.N.
    In 1990, she goes on to become a Senior Lecturer and in 1996, she is made a Consultant Pharmacologist at the College of Medicine.
    In 1996, Akunyili becomes Zonal Secretary of the Petroleum Special Trust Fund (P.T.F), coordinating projects funded by profits from oil in Nigeria's South Eastern States.

  • 2001: She is appointed Director-General of NAFDAC by the then Presient Olusegun Obasanjo.

  • 2001-2008: She serves as Director-General of NAFDAC.During her tenure, Dora saw to the reduction of counterfeit drug being sold in the country by about 80%.An impressive feat as an individual in the political landsape of Nigeria.
    As of June, 2006, she was reported to have secured convictions for 45 counterfeiters with 56 cases pending. Her efforts led to increased public awareness about counterfeit drugs and more direct and purposeful surveillance at Nigerian customs.

  • 2008: She is appointed Minister of Information and Communications.

  • 2010: On December 16, She resigns from her position as Minister to run for office as the Senatir representing Anambra Central in the National Assembly.

  • 2011: She runs for election as Senator for Anambra Central for the APGA in April but is defeated by Chris Ngige of the ACN.

  • 2014: On the 7th of June, Dora Nkem Akunyili dies at a hospital in India after a 2 year battle with uterine cancer.
